Class 6 Class 9 OMR Based Conducted by NVS
Duration2 Hours
FrequencyOnce a Year
Negative MarkingNo
MediumEnglish / Hindi / Regional

Paper Pattern — Class 6 (JNVST)

Section Questions Marks Duration
Mental Ability Test (MAT) 40 50 60 min
Arithmetic Test 20 25 30 min
Language Test 20 25 30 min
Total 80 100 2 Hours
The MAT section carries 50% weightage. It is entirely non-verbal (diagrams & patterns), so no reading ability is needed for this section — making it accessible to all linguistic backgrounds.

Detailed Syllabus

Mental Ability Test (Non-Verbal)
  • Odd Figure Out
  • Figure Matching & Pattern Completion
  • Figure Series Completion
  • Analogy (Geometric Pattern)
  • Geometrical Figure Completion
  • Mirror Images
  • Punched Hole / Paper Folding
  • Space Visualization
Arithmetic Test
  • Number & Numeric System
  • Four Fundamental Operations
  • LCM & HCF
  • Fractions & Decimals
  • Percentage, Profit & Loss
  • Simple Interest
  • Ratio & Proportion
  • Time, Distance & Work
  • Area, Perimeter, Volume
  • Approximation
Language Test
  • Reading Comprehension (2 Passages)
  • Cloze Test (Fill in Blanks in Passage)
  • Vocabulary Usage
  • Correct Use of Words in Context
  • Grammar & Usage
Pro Tip: JNVST Class 6 is unique — practice specifically Non-Verbal/Diagram based patterns. Students from any board (CBSE, State) are equally placed here since MAT has zero text.

Detailed Syllabus

Physics
  • Kinematics & Laws of Motion
  • Work, Power & Energy
  • Rotational Motion & Gravitation
  • Properties of Matter & Thermodynamics
  • Waves & Oscillations
  • Electrostatics & Capacitance
  • Current Electricity & Magnetism
  • Electromagnetic Induction & AC Circuits
  • Ray Optics & Wave Optics
  • Modern Physics & Semiconductors
Chemistry
  • Physical: Mole Concept, Thermodynamics, Equilibrium
  • Physical: Electrochemistry, Chemical Kinetics
  • Physical: Solutions, Surface Chemistry
  • Inorganic: Periodic Table & Bonding
  • Inorganic: s, p, d, f Block Elements
  • Inorganic: Coordination Chemistry
  • Organic: GOC, Hydrocarbons, Haloalkanes
  • Organic: Alcohols, Amines, Carbonyl Compounds
  • Organic: Biomolecules & Polymers
Mathematics
  • Sets, Relations & Functions
  • Complex Numbers & Quadratic Equations
  • Matrices, Determinants & Permutations
  • Sequences, Series & Binomial Theorem
  • Limits, Continuity & Differentiability
  • Application of Derivatives & Integrals
  • Differential Equations
  • Coordinate Geometry (2D & 3D)
  • Vectors & 3D Geometry
  • Probability & Statistics
  • Trigonometry
Pro Tip: For JEE Advanced, focus heavily on multi-concept problems. NCERT alone is sufficient only for Chemistry theory; Physics and Maths require advanced problem books (e.g., H.C. Verma, Irodov, Arihant).

NEET (UG)

Medical UG Pen & Paper Conducted by NTA
Duration3 Hours 20 Min
FrequencyOnce a Year (May)
Negative Marking−1 per wrong answer
Medium13 Languages

Paper Pattern

Subject Section A Section B Total Marks Marking
Physics 35 Qs (compulsory) 15 Qs (attempt 10) 180 +4 / −1

Botany 35 Qs (compulsory) 15 Qs (attempt 10) 180
Zoology 35 Qs (compulsory) 15 Qs (attempt 10) 180

Detailed Syllabus

Physics (Class 11 + 12)
  • Physical World, Units & Measurement
  • Kinematics & Laws of Motion
  • Work, Energy, Power & Gravitation
  • Properties of Bulk Matter & Thermodynamics
  • Oscillations & Waves
  • Electrostatics, Current Electricity, Magnetism
  • EM Induction, AC, EM Waves
  • Optics & Dual Nature of Matter
  • Atoms, Nuclei & Electronic Devices
Chemistry (Class 11 + 12)
  • Basic Concepts, Structure of Atom, Bonding
  • States of Matter, Thermodynamics, Equilibrium
  • Redox, Hydrogen, s-Block & Organic Chemistry basics
  • Solid State, Solutions, Electrochemistry
  • Chemical Kinetics, Surface Chemistry
  • p, d & f Block Elements, Coordination Compounds
  • Alcohols, Phenols, Ethers, Aldehydes, Ketones
  • Amines, Biomolecules, Polymers, Chemistry in Everyday Life
Botany (Class 11 + 12)
  • Diversity of Living World (Plant Kingdom)
  • Morphology & Anatomy of Flowering Plants
  • Cell Biology & Cell Division
  • Plant Physiology: Transport, Mineral Nutrition
  • Photosynthesis & Respiration in Plants
  • Sexual Reproduction in Flowering Plants
  • Genetics: Principles of Inheritance & Variation
  • Molecular Basis of Inheritance
  • Ecology & Environment
Zoology (Class 11 + 12)
  • Animal Kingdom: Classification
  • Structural Organisation in Animals
  • Human Physiology (Digestion, Respiration, Circulation)
  • Excretion, Locomotion & Neural Control
  • Chemical Coordination & Integration
  • Human Reproduction & Reproductive Health
  • Evolution & Human Health & Disease
  • Microbes in Human Welfare
  • Biotechnology & Biodiversity
Pro Tip: NEET Biology carries 360/720 marks (50% of total). NCERT lines are directly quoted in questions — read NCERT Biology word-for-word, especially Chapters on Genetics, Ecology, and Human Physiology.

UG Central Universities Computer Based Conducted by NTA
Duration45 min per section
FrequencyOnce a Year (May–June)
Negative Marking−1 per wrong answer
Covers200+ Central/State Universities

Exam Structure

Section Description Questions Attempt Marks/Q
Section IA 13 Languages (Reading, Literary Aptitude, Vocab) 50 40 +5 / −1
Section IB 20 Additional Languages 50 40 +5 / −1
Section II 27 Domain Subjects (Class 12 NCERT Syllabus) 50 40 +5 / −1

Section-Wise Syllabus

Section IA — Language
  • Reading Comprehension: Factual, Narrative, Literary

  • Choosing Appropriate Word/Expression

Section II — Domain (Examples)
  • Physics / Chemistry / Biology / Maths — Class 12 NCERT
  • Accountancy, Business Studies, Economics
  • History, Political Science, Geography, Sociology

  • General Knowledge & Current Affairs
  • General Mental Ability & Reasoning
  • Numerical Ability (Class 8 Level)
  • Quantitative Reasoning
  • Logical & Analytical Reasoning
Pro Tip: Each university specifies which CUET sections they consider. Check your target university's requirements before selecting domain subjects. For DU, most courses need Language + 1–3 Domain subjects. Section III General Test helps in many humanities courses.

Olympiads — SOF (NSO, IMO, IEO, NCO)

Class 1–12 OMR Based SOF / Silverzone / HBCSE
Duration60 Minutes
StagesLevel 1 → Level 2 (Top scorers)
Negative MarkingNo
Achievers Section3 Marks per question

SOF Olympiad — General Pattern (Class 3–10)

Section Questions Marks/Q Total
Section 1 — Logical Reasoning 15 1 15
Section 2 — Subject Knowledge (Core) 25 1 25
Section 3 — Everyday Mathematics / Science 10 1 10
Section 4 — Achievers Section 5 3 15
Total 55 — 65

Subject-Wise Overview

IMO — International Maths Olympiad
  • Logical Reasoning (Number & Letter Puzzles)
  • Mathematical Reasoning (Based on Current Grade Maths)
  • Everyday Mathematics (Word Problem Application)
  • Achievers Section: High-order thinking maths problems
  • Syllabus = Current grade NCERT/CBSE Maths
NSO — National Science Olympiad
  • Logical Reasoning (Non-verbal + Verbal)
  • Science: Physics, Chemistry, Biology (Current Grade)
  • Science in Everyday Life
  • Achievers: Application-based science problems
  • Syllabus = Current grade NCERT Science
IEO — International English Olympiad
  • Word & Structure Knowledge (Grammar, Vocabulary)
  • Reading (Comprehension passages)
  • Spoken & Written Expression
  • Achievers: High-Order Thinking Language Questions
NCO — National Cyber Olympiad
  • Logical Reasoning
  • Computers & IT (Hardware, Software, Internet basics)
  • Computer Science (Programming basics for higher grades)
  • Achievers: Higher-Order Thinking
HBCSE Olympiads (IMO, IPhO, IChO, INBiO, IOAA) are national-level olympiads for Classes 9–12, far more rigorous than SOF. Selection is through PRMO → RMO → INMO → IMO (for Maths). These test deep conceptual understanding beyond any board curriculum.
Pro Tip: The Achievers Section (5 questions × 3 marks = 15 marks) is a rank-differentiator. Most students score similarly on Sections 1–3. Mastering the Achievers questions with high-order thinking problems determines Gold vs Silver ranks.
